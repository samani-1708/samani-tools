Split PDF FAQs.
How can I split one PDF into multiple documents?

The Acrobat Split PDF online tool lets you quickly split and separate PDF pages into up to 20 new PDF files without downloading additional software.
Here are the steps to split a PDF using the Acrobat Split PDF online tool:
1. Start by selecting a PDF of 1,500 pages or less and sign in to Acrobat.
2. Then, insert as many as 19 divider lines to determine the number of pages desired in each split PDF file.
3. Finally, click Save.
Acrobat will split PDF into pages based on the divider lines and save your new split PDF files on your device.
What is the maximum PDF file size I can split?

You can choose to separate pages in PDFs with a maximum file size of 1GB using our online PDF splitter. Simply drag and drop the files into our PDF splitter tool using the instructions above.
How do I save new PDF files after I split a PDF?

After you select pages and set up separator lines, click Save. Acrobat saves the split PDFs in an Adobe cloud storage folder within your Acrobat account. You can rename, download or share the new PDFs with others.

You can also convert images like PNGs or JPGs and convert PDFs to and from Microsoft Word, PowerPoint (PPT) and Excel.
Can I use PDFs offline without an internet connection?
Yes, you can split PDF pages offline without an internet connection with an Acrobat Pro subscription and downloading the Acrobat software. Once downloaded, you can easily split PDFs into multiple documents or edit them as you wish.
